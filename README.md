## What It Documents

This repository presents the public record, technical context, inventor listing, institutional context and reference sources for a biotechnology patent family related to nucleotide mixtures used in nucleic acid amplification and sequencing.

The invention describes mixtures of nucleotides, including dNTPs and/or analogues, in non-equimolar proportions or molar concentrations. These mixtures are proposed for nucleic acid polymerization workflows such as PCR, RT-PCR, cDNA synthesis and sequencing.

The core technical idea is that DNA/RNA targets do not all have the same nucleotide composition. A conventional equimolar nucleotide mixture may be suboptimal for some templates, especially when nucleotide proportions or GC/AT content differ substantially. The patent proposes adjusting nucleotide mixture composition according to the polymer being amplified or sequenced.

## Why It Matters

Nucleic acid amplification and sequencing are foundational techniques in molecular biology, diagnostics, biotechnology and biomedical research. Their reliability depends not only on enzymes and thermal conditions, but also on reagent composition.

This patent family frames nucleotide mixture design as an adjustable experimental variable. That makes the invention relevant to reaction optimization and to the broader idea that molecular workflows can be tuned to the biological composition of the target.
