# Technical Background

## Nucleotides And Polymerization

DNA polymerization depends on the availability of nucleotide triphosphates. In a standard PCR reaction, the four canonical deoxynucleotide triphosphates are usually supplied together:

- dATP
- dTTP
- dGTP
- dCTP

RNA-related workflows may involve uracil-containing components or reverse-transcription steps, depending on the method.

## Why Mixture Composition Matters

An equimolar mixture gives each nucleotide the same starting concentration. That is convenient for general use, but biological templates are not always compositionally balanced. Some regions are GC-rich, others are AT-rich, and some targets have distinctive nucleotide distributions.

The patent's technical premise is that nucleotide availability can be tuned to the composition of the polymer being amplified or sequenced. In that sense, the invention belongs to the practical interface between molecular biology and reaction optimization.

## Where The Idea Applies

The patent record describes applications including:

- PCR;
- RT-PCR;
- cDNA synthesis;
- conventional sequencing;
- pyrosequencing;
- third-generation sequencing contexts.

## Conceptual Importance

The invention is not merely about adding nucleotides to a reaction. It is about treating nucleotide mixture design as a variable that can be adapted to the molecular target.

That makes the patent relevant to a broader principle in molecular biology: experimental reagents are not neutral defaults; they encode assumptions about the system being measured or amplified.
